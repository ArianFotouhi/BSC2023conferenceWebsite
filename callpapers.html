Call for Papers

The 2023 31st Biennial Symposium on Communications (BSC 2023) is a prestigious international research conference in communications, information theory, and signal processing. The Symposium is presented by the Canadian Society of Information Theory, and will be held in Montreal, Quebec, Canada from June 26 to June 29, 2023. The event will feature several diverse activities including keynote speeches, tutorials, and so on.

Keynote Speakers

  • Sherman Shen, University of Waterloo, Canada
  • Olgica Milenkovic, UIUC, USA
  • Octavia A. Dobre, Memorial University, Canada
  • Marco Di Renzo, Université Paris-Saclay, France

Submission Guidelines

BSC 2023 solicits previously unpublished contributions from a broad range of topics in communications, information theory, and signal processing. The scope of BSC 2023 encompasses, but is not limited to:
  • Communication and Information Theory
  • Coding and Signal Processing for Communications
  • MIMIO and Cooperative Communications
  • Millimeter Wave Communication
  • Reconfigurable Intelligent Surfaces
  • Source coding and Multimedia Communications
  • Communication QoS, Reliability and Modeling
  • Mobile & Wireless Network
  • Internet of Things (IoT) and Sensor Networks
  • Satellite, Space, and Aerial Communications
  • Machine Learning for Communications and Networks
  • Green Communication Systems and Networks
  • Next Generation Networking & Internet
  • Smart Grid Communications
  • Optical Communication Networks and Systems
  • Nano-networking and Molecular Communication
  • Software-Defined Networking
  • Network Virtualization
  • Edge/Fog/Cloud Computing
  • Communication System Security and Cryptography
